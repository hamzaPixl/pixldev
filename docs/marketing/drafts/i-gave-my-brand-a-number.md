_Most brand identity lives as a vibe in a founder's head. I decided to sample mine, score it, and lock the number into everything I generate._

---

Ask a founder for their brand colors.

You'll get a hex code they half-remember, a logo file from two redesigns ago, and a strong feeling that it's "kind of green, but warmer." Then every new asset gets eyeballed against that feeling, and slowly the brand drifts.

I didn't want a feeling. I wanted a number. So I built the thing that measures it.

## Guessed vs measured

The usual way to define a brand is to guess it. Someone opens a design tool, picks a swatch that looks right, writes it into a guideline nobody reads, and calls it canonical.

The problem is the source of truth is a memory. Memories drift. Six months later the "brand green" on the site, in the deck, and in the last ad are three different greens, and nobody can say which one is correct because none of them were ever measured.

So I flipped it. **The live site is the source of truth, not the guideline.** Whatever is actually rendering to real visitors is, by definition, the brand. Measure that.

## How the measuring works

The shape of it, without handing over the whole blueprint.

→ Load the real site, the one people actually see.
→ Sample the colors pixel by pixel, not from a style guide but from the rendered pixels.
→ Score them into a small, ordered palette: the accent, the near-black, the supporting tones.

The output isn't a mood board. It's a short list of exact values with weights, derived from what's live. A machine-readable answer to "what does this brand actually look like right now."

That's the part that matters: the palette is sampled, not chosen. If the site changes, the measurement changes with it. The brand can't drift away from itself, because the definition is downstream of the reality instead of the other way around.

## Then lock it into generation

A measured palette that sits in a file is just a nicer guideline. The point is what happens next.

The number gets locked into every generation. When I produce an image, a video frame, an asset, the accent isn't a swatch someone remembered. It's the value sampled from the live site, fed into the same manifest that drives everything else. Measured once, applied everywhere.

This is how Bumpi works, in one line: measured, not guessed. The brand is a reading, and the reading is what generation obeys.

## The honest state

Here's the caveat, because a number can lie too.

A palette is the easy, objective part of a brand. Color has coordinates. Voice, taste, and the reason a layout feels right do not sample as cleanly, and I'm not going to pretend a color scorer captures those. It captures the part that's measurable and leaves the rest to judgment.

So the number isn't the whole brand. It's the part I refuse to keep guessing about, when guessing was never necessary.

Stop remembering your brand. Sample it.
